The Challenge

Norway is falling behind in the race
for productions

While neighboring countries strengthen their film incentives, Norway stands still. The result is that productions that could create value here move abroad.

Lowest in the Nordics

Norway's incentive scheme at 25% is the lowest among Nordic countries, and well below the European average.

Lack of predictability

The scheme has no guarantee of sufficient funding, making it impossible to plan large-scale productions.

Productions are fleeing

International productions choose neighboring countries over Norway — and with them go jobs and expertise.

Lost value creation

Billions in potential revenue, tourism and cultural exports are lost every single year.

Research & Data

What the reports show

Four key reports document the value of a strengthened film incentive scheme — and the consequences of standing still.

Olsberg SPI

Global Incentives Index 2025

Olsberg SPI's biannual overview of global film incentives shows that Norway at 25% sits significantly below the European average. Iceland (40%), Ireland (35%) and Finland (30%) compete fiercely for the same productions.

Read more →
Government

Norway Incentive Impact Study 2023

The government's own impact analysis documents that for every krone invested in the incentive scheme, 4.7 kroner in socioeconomic value is generated. At least 1.9 kroner return directly through taxes and fees.

Our Demands

Four measures that will change everything

Concrete measures that will make Norway competitive and ensure a sustainable film industry.

01
◆

Predictability

The scheme must have sufficient and predictable funding so that productions can plan long-term. Today's uncertainty scares away investments.

02
◆

International competitiveness

The incentive rate must be increased to a minimum of 30% to match neighboring countries. 25% is not enough when Iceland offers 40% and Finland 30%.

03
◆

Access for Norwegian projects

Norwegian productions with international potential must also be able to use the scheme, not just foreign productions.

04
◆

Sustainable production

The incentive scheme should promote environmentally friendly film production and help the Norwegian industry lead the way in green transition.
